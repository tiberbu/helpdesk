# Tech-Spec: Helpdesk Feature Roadmap & Business Requirements Document

**Created:** 2026-03-21

---

## Overview

### Problem Statement

Frappe Helpdesk is a functional but feature-incomplete support platform. It lacks 17 critical capabilities that every major competitor already provides — including canned responses, collision detection, CSAT surveys, live chat, and AI-powered features. Without a comprehensive, structured roadmap and business requirements document, development efforts will remain fragmented, prioritization will be ad-hoc, and the platform cannot reach competitive parity with Zendesk, Freshdesk, or Intercom. There is no single authoritative document guiding where the product should go, why, and in what order.

### Solution

Synthesize all prior research (competitive analysis of 15 tools, ITIL framework requirements, codebase audit findings) into two master planning documents:

1. **`docs/feature-roadmap.md`** — A phased, prioritized feature roadmap spanning 4 phases across 36 months, with every feature tagged by ITIL practice, competitive benchmark, effort estimate, and business value
2. **`docs/BRD.md`** — A formal Business Requirements Document covering executive summary, problem statement, business objectives, stakeholder map, functional requirements (by ITIL practice), non-functional requirements, integration requirements, success criteria, and risk register

These documents serve as the **single source of truth** for all future development, sprint planning, and stakeholder communication.

### Scope

**In Scope:**
- Feature roadmap across 4 phases (Foundation, Intelligence, Excellence, Innovation)
- Business Requirements Document with full ITIL-aligned functional requirements
- Vision statement and differentiator analysis
- Competitive benchmarking per feature
- Effort and business value estimates for every feature
- Non-functional requirements (performance, security, scalability, usability)
- Integration requirements (Frappe ecosystem, channels, AI/LLM, third-party)
- Success criteria per phase with measurable KPIs
- Risk register with mitigations

**Out of Scope:**
- Implementation code for any feature
- Sprint planning or ticket creation
- Technical architecture diagrams beyond what exists in competitive analysis
- Pricing or monetization strategy
- Legal/compliance certification processes
- Mobile native app specification (separate spec needed)

### Technical Decisions

1. **ITIL as the organizing framework** — All functional requirements in the BRD are grouped by ITIL practice (not by UI area or technical area). This aligns with enterprise buying criteria and ensures completeness.

2. **4-phase structure** — Phase 1 (Foundation, months 1–6) addresses the most embarrassing gaps (canned responses, collision detection, CSAT, live chat). This is deliberately minimal to ship fast. Phase 2 (Intelligence) introduces AI. Phase 3 (Excellence) adds ITSM. Phase 4 (Innovation) creates category-defining features.

3. **P0/P1/P2/P3/P4 priority scheme** — P0 = ship in month 1–2 (no exceptions), P1 = Phase 1, P2 = Phase 2, P3 = Phase 3, P4 = Phase 4. This maps directly to the roadmap phases.

4. **Open-source AI as a core differentiator** — Local LLM support (Ollama, LM Studio) is a first-class citizen in the architecture, not an afterthought. This enables $0/resolution AI for self-hosted customers vs. Zendesk's $1.00/resolution.

5. **ERPNext integration as primary moat** — No competitor can match native ERP data in the agent workspace. This is emphasized throughout the roadmap as the #1 unique differentiator.

### Notes

**Key insight from research:** The most critical P0 gaps (canned responses, internal notes, collision detection) are all UI/UX features with relatively low backend complexity. They should be the very first development sprint — they are embarrassing gaps that cost Frappe Helpdesk evaluation wins daily. None require AI, new infrastructure, or architectural changes.

**Strategic positioning:** "The open-source helpdesk with AI superpowers — no per-agent fees, no vendor lock-in, endlessly customizable." This positioning owns the intersection of three market gaps: SMBs priced out of Zendesk, enterprises demanding data sovereignty, and developer-oriented teams that want to build on (not just configure) their helpdesk.

**Competitive moat priority:** The ERPNext deep integration (native order/invoice/asset data in agent workspace) is the single most defensible competitive advantage. No SaaS helpdesk can replicate it. This must be the first integration built in Phase 1 alongside the basic feature gaps.

**AI architecture decision:** Local LLM support (via Ollama) should be the default AI path, with cloud LLMs as opt-in. This preserves data privacy, eliminates per-resolution costs, and serves the self-hosting customer segment that is Frappe's core audience. The cost comparison is compelling: Zendesk at $1.00/resolution × 10,000 resolutions/month = $10,000/month vs. Frappe on-prem at $0/resolution.

**Phase gating risk:** The most common failure mode for roadmaps is Phase 3 scope creep bleeding into Phase 1 timelines. Recommend strict quarterly roadmap reviews with a "feature freeze" policy: no Phase 2+ features start until 80% of Phase 1 ACs are met.
